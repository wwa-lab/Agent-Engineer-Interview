# Behavioral Interview Questions

Answer with a specific story. Do not share confidential prompts or identifying details; describe the engineering situation at a safe level.

## Interview Question

Tell me about a time an AI feature failed in a way the team did not anticipate.

## Difficulty

Intermediate to Staff

## Type

Behavioral

## Follow-up Questions

What did you personally own? What changed afterward? How did you know the fix worked?

### Q1. Tell me about a project that failed

- **Difficulty:** Intermediate · **Type:** Behavioral · **Time:** 4 minutes
- **Core check:** Ownership and learning.
- **Strong shape:** Explain the original hypothesis, the evidence that disproved it, your decision, and the process change—not a blame narrative.
- **Common mistake:** Calling a project failure only a model problem.
- **Follow-up:** What would you stop earlier next time?

### Q2. Tell me about a hallucination incident

- **Difficulty:** Intermediate · **Type:** Behavioral · **Time:** 4 minutes
- **Core check:** Safety response.
- **Strong shape:** Describe impact, containment, user communication, root cause, regression test, and prevention. Remove identifying details.
- **Common mistake:** Presenting a prompt tweak as the full mitigation.
- **Follow-up:** Which layer should have caught it?

### Q3. Tell me about a time costs exceeded plan

- **Difficulty:** Intermediate · **Type:** Behavioral · **Time:** 4 minutes
- **Core check:** Operational ownership.
- **Strong shape:** Quantify the budget gap, decompose cost by step, choose a quality-preserving change, and document the new budget alert.
- **Common mistake:** Switching to a cheaper model without evaluating quality.
- **Follow-up:** What cost would you not optimize away?

### Q4. Tell me about handling prompt injection

- **Difficulty:** Senior · **Type:** Behavioral · **Time:** 4 minutes
- **Core check:** Security judgment.
- **Strong shape:** Show how you modeled untrusted content, reduced authority, tested the attack path, and communicated residual risk.
- **Common mistake:** Claiming the system is injection-proof.
- **Follow-up:** What defense failed first?

### Q5. Tell me about a cross-team disagreement

- **Difficulty:** Intermediate · **Type:** Behavioral · **Time:** 4 minutes
- **Core check:** Influence through evidence.
- **Strong shape:** State the competing goals, create a shared experiment or decision record, and explain how the relationship and outcome were protected.
- **Common mistake:** Making the other team sound unreasonable.
- **Follow-up:** What did you learn from their position?

### Q6. Tell me about a production outage

- **Difficulty:** Senior · **Type:** Behavioral · **Time:** 4 minutes
- **Core check:** Incident management.
- **Strong shape:** Cover detection, mitigation, communication, recovery, and follow-up actions. Separate facts known during the incident from later analysis.
- **Common mistake:** Spending the story on implementation detail and skipping users.
- **Follow-up:** Which alert would you add?

### Q7. Tell me about an ambiguous requirement

- **Difficulty:** Intermediate · **Type:** Behavioral · **Time:** 4 minutes
- **Core check:** Clarification and delivery.
- **Strong shape:** Identify the unknowns, propose a narrow testable slice, align on success, and show what you shipped while uncertainty remained.
- **Common mistake:** Waiting for perfect requirements.
- **Follow-up:** What assumption was riskiest?

### Q8. Tell me about choosing a model

- **Difficulty:** Intermediate · **Type:** Behavioral · **Time:** 4 minutes
- **Core check:** Decision quality.
- **Strong shape:** Explain criteria, test set, privacy and cost constraints, stakeholder input, and the rollback or re-evaluation plan.
- **Common mistake:** Citing a leaderboard without task evidence.
- **Follow-up:** What result would have changed the choice?

### Q9. Tell me about build vs buy

- **Difficulty:** Senior · **Type:** Behavioral · **Time:** 4 minutes
- **Core check:** Strategic trade-offs.
- **Strong shape:** Compare differentiation, security, integration, total cost, lock-in, and ownership. Describe how you made the decision reversible where possible.
- **Common mistake:** Treating build as more controllable by default.
- **Follow-up:** What exit criteria did you define?

### Q10. Tell me about a time you improved a system without a flashy demo

- **Difficulty:** Intermediate · **Type:** Behavioral · **Time:** 4 minutes
- **Core check:** Engineering maturity.
- **Strong shape:** Quantify a reliability, latency, evaluation, cost, or developer-experience improvement and explain the invisible work that made it safe.
- **Common mistake:** Measuring only activity or lines of code.
- **Follow-up:** How did you get adoption?
